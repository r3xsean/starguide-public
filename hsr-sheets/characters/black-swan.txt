NAME: BLACK SWAN

MAIN ROLE: DPS/Support
DAMAGE TYPE: DoT
DEBUFF TYPE: DEF Shred, Vulnerability, DoT
TEAM ARCHETYPES: DoT, Debuff
SP USAGE: Depends on SP amount
SKILL ROTATION: E spam or EAEA or EAA

=== GENERAL ANALYSIS ===
Kit Overview: Damage over Time DPS
Black Swan inflicts [Arcana] on enemies through her Basic Attack, Skill, or whenever an enemy received DoT damage. [Arcana] is considered Wind Shear , and it deals more damage with more stacks. [Arcana] also has unique effects depending on the amount of stacks. After enemy turn [Arcana] damage on their turn, [Arcana] reset to 1

Black Swan's Ultimate deals AoE damage and inflict Epiphany, which has these following effects: Increase damage taken, prevent [Arcana] reset once.

Synergies:
- Units that can frequently applies and detonate DoT, such as Kafka or Sampo.
- Harmony characters, especially one that can provide team buffs or AoE debuffers

Tips:
- Black Swan's vulnerability from her ult only works during enemies turn. Play around it to maximize your team's damage.
- Black Swan can also function as a support outside of DoT team, thanks to her minor DEF shreds on Skill and Vulnerability on Ultimate, although this pale in comparison with dedicated 5* support.

=== WORTH PULLING FOR ===
- If you like the character, pull them!

- Black Swan & Kafka is a strong duo that can tackles most end game fights. They are worth pulling together if you enjoy the DoT playstyle.

- Although Black Swan has viability as a support, it is not worth to pull her just for that.

=== E0S0 VIABILITY ===
Yes, but with Kafka

Kafka is mandatory for Black Swan to function well

=== VERTICAL INVESTMENT ORDER ===
Kafka > E1 > S1

=== VERTICAL INVESTMENT EXPLANATION ===
While Black Swan has good DoT damage, she has no methods to detonate them. Therefore, Kafka is a must for Black Swan to function well, as she's the only available DoT detonator.

E1 allows Black Swan to inflict Type RES shred of the DoT type on the enemies

S1 increases EHR% and whenever the wearer deals damage to enemy, they gain ATK and their DoT will ignore DEF. The more DoT type the enemy has, the stronger the effect.
